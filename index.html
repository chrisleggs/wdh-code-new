Atmospheric forecasts

Predictions of future weather conditions based on scientific measurements of the atmosphere and computer models that show how it is expected to change.

Global Deterministic 10km Forecast

A numerical weather prediction forecast for the whole globe, with a resolution of approximately 0.09 degrees i.e. 10km (2,560 x 1,920 grid points).

Site-specific forecasts

Weather predictions tailored for a single location, giving detailed conditions exactly where they matter rather than for a broad region.

Global Spot Forecast

Gives a “most likely” forecast for over 20,000 specific locations worldwide by combining several weather models and recent observations. It uses gridded atmospheric model data (including 10 km global and 2 km UK model inputs) to produce consistent site-level forecasts.

Forecast length:
7 days (168 hours)
Update frequency:
Hourly
Find out more

Site-Specific Blended Probabilistic Forecast

Blends multiple weather models to show the probability of different weather outcomes at a single location, along with a “most likely” forecast. It provides probability and percentile forecasts out to seven days, helping users make risk-based decisions.

Forecast length:
7 days (168 hours)
Update frequency:
Hourly

Observations

Regular measurements of weather taken from instruments at ground-based locations, recording things like temperature, rainfall, wind, and sunshine.

UK Land Observations

Provides hourly real-world weather measurements from around 150 UK stations spaced roughly 50 km apart. Sensors follow strict international standards, and data is automatically recorded and transmitted for reliable surface-level conditions.
